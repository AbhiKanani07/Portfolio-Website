Case Study

YouTube Recommendation Bias & Viewing Behavior Model

Built a graph-based “baby YouTube algorithm” using my own watch history to see how recommendations influence what I watch next.

Why it fits

  • I ran a YouTube channel growing up, so I’ve long thought about how recommendations drive clicks and retention.
  • Now I can pair that experience with data analysis to quantify how the algorithm nudges my behavior.

Data & parsing

  • Used YouTube watch-history export (JSON/HTML) for timestamps, titles, channels, and inferred categories.
  • Built Pandas pipelines to clean, categorize, and enrich sessions with watch-time and return-visit flags.

Analyses

  • Watch time per category and channel “return” rates
  • Topic transitions: what I watch after each category or channel
  • Preference drift over years and session-level binge vs. casual patterns

Recommendation simulator

  • Represented videos as nodes and transitions as edges; estimated “likely next video” probabilities.
  • Simple graph-based model to simulate what the feed might surface next given recent views.

Optional modeling

  • Predict daily watch time given recent behavior
  • Topic clustering to identify core interest groups
  • Detect binge sessions and measure topic drift

Interview angle

I analyzed my own history to understand how algorithms steer behavior, then built a transition-probability model to illustrate that influence. Metrics like topic drift and binge likelihood make the story concrete, memorable, and personal.
